About

I have worked in different biological and medical areas throughout my career. In my Master studies, I found that antibody heavy chains already confer DNA recognition. This opens new doors for research in systemic lupus erythematosus, since these patients have anti-DNA antibodies. Afterwards I studied evolution of ncRNA structures during my PhD studies. Among other things, I developed the first tool in the scientific community that can detect positive selection in ncRNA structures. My tool, the SSS-test, allowed me to further characterize a few candidates that are relevant for neurodegenerative diseases. I have found that they have local strucures that are fast-evolving in the human species.

In addition to these findings, I specialized in communicating proficiently with researchers from different fields and backgrounds. Effective communication creates an ideal environment to problem solving.

In order to be productive and objective in my work, I have developed my own organizational framework. This is based on a personal lab book, which I write in Latex. I also use GIT daily, a version control tool, for updating my lab book and codes. As an example of how efficient my framework is, I managed to write my PhD dissertation in only 170 hours, which is a very short time for such a task.
