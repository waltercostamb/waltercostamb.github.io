About

I studied biological sciences in Brazil at the University of Brasília from 2006 to 2009. Afterwards I did my Masters at the molecular biology department also at the University of Brasília from 2010 to 2012. From 2013 to 2018 I completed my PhD studies in Germany at the faculty of mathematics and computer science of the University of Leipzig. My research focus at the PhD was evolution of non-coding RNA structures. This was a theoretical work, in which I developed new tools and analysed primate data. I also worked on projects in immunology, metagenomics and industrial improvement of microorganisms.

I have worked in Brazil as well as Germany with multi-cultural teams. I specialized in communicating proficiently with researchers from different fields, including biology, biochemistry, computer science and statistics. I can translate the ideas of a biological problem into structured sub-problems that can be tackled with computational techniques. In addition, I can also communicate the technical results to researchers from biology. This creates an efficient environment to problem solving.

During my career as a bioinformatician, I have developed my own organizational framework. I write down every task I do every day in my personal lab book, which I write in Latex. This makes it easier and faster to transfer blocks of text to other documents, such as reports or presentations. In addition to that, I save all new versions of my "lab-book.tex" document in GIT, along with my codes, so that I can easily transfer my work to different computers. I also take time to plan my week and organize it into Trello cards or bullet points onto a paper notebook. My framework optimizes my time and boosts my productivity. I invite you to take a look at the visual scheme below and try my Latex tutorial to start your own Lab Book in Latex.
