About

I am an applied Bioinformatician and have worked in different biological and medical areas. In my Master studies, I researched the genetic variability of anti-DNA antibodies and found that part of the antibody heavy chain already confer DNA recognition. This opens new doors for research in systemic lupus erythematosus, since these patients have anti-DNA antibodies. Afterwards, from 2013 to 2018 I studied evolution of ncRNA structures during my PhD studies. Among other things, I developed the first tool in the scientific community that can detect positive selection in ncRNA structures. My tool, the SSS-test, allowed me to further characterize a few candidates that are relevant for neurodegenerative diseases. I have found that they have local strucures that are fast-evolving in the human species.

Apart from these results, I have worked in Brazil, Germany and Mexico with multi-cultural teams. I specialized in communicating proficiently with researchers from different fields and backgrounds, which is quite challenging. Problem-solving communication creates an efficient environment to problem solving.

During my career, I have developed my own organizational framework, based on a personal lab book, which I write in Latex. Along with GIT, a version control tool, for my lab book and my codes, I found a very efficient and productive way to work. Using this framework, I managed to write my PhD dissertation in only 170 hours, which is a very short time.
