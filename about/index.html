About

I have worked in different biological and medical areas throughout my career. In my Master studies, I found that antibody heavy chains already confer DNA recognition. This opens new doors for research in systemic lupus erythematosus, since these patients have anti-DNA antibodies.

Afterwards, in my PhD studies, I further characterized a few biomarkers that are relevant for neurodegenerative diseases. This was possible with my software 'SSS-test', the first one in the scientific community that can detect positive selection in ncRNA structures. I detected fast-evolving local strucures in those biomarkers of neurodegenerative disease, which helps us to better understand them.

In addition to these scientific findings, I specialized in communicating proficiently with researchers from different fields and backgrounds. Effective communication creates an ideal environment to problem solving.

In order to be productive and objective in my work, I have developed my own organizational framework. This is based on a personal lab book, which I write in Latex. I also use GIT daily, a version control tool, for updating my lab book and my codes. As an example of how efficient my framework is, I managed to write my PhD dissertation in only 170 hours, which is a very short time for such a task.
