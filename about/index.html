Projects

On this page I highlight selected projects in the field of Bioinformatics and Computational Biology.

Machine Learning in Microbiology

I led and developed a research project in Microbiology as a research associate in the Microverse Cluster at the University of Jena, within the Viral Ecology and Omics group led by Prof. Dr. Bas E. Dutilh. High throughput genomic feature extraction reveals prokaryotic adaptations to the abiotic environment I analyzed 13,554 microbial genomes to understand their adaptation to environmental conditions. For this, I developed Machine Learning models (classification and regression) to predict growth levels of salinity, temperature, oxygen, and pH preferences. I also built FxTractor, a scalable high-throughput pipeline for extracting genomic features using Python, Snakemake and HPC. My predictions were validated with experimental data from a new extremophilic isolate. In this project, I could identify molecular signatures linked to environmental adaptation, including protein-coding genes and ncRNAs. These results improve the understanding of microbe–environment interactions and highlight potential biotechnological applications. In this project, I led the analysis, coordinated the collaborations and developed the models and tools.

Results: 1 published scientific article, 2 scientific articles in development, 2 tools published on GitHub, Co-supervision of a PhD student, Development and teaching of one Masters-level course on Big Data Analysis of Viromes

Statistical Analysis in Laboratory Medicine

I contributed to three research projects in laboratory medicine as part of the AMPEL project at the University Hospital Leipzig, in the group led by Prof. Dr. med. Torsten Kaiser.

  • Focus: Statistical evaluation of clinical and laboratory data
  • Methods: [e.g., regression models, survival analysis, R/Python]
  • Role: Data analysis, study design support
  • Outcome: [e.g., clinical insights, publications, decision support]

In my PhD studies, I developed different methods and algorithms to study evolution of non-coding RNAs. Most prominently, I developed the SSS-test, the first test in the scientific community that can detect positive selection in ncRNA structures. With the SSS-test, it is possible to detect fast-evolving strucures in any biological system.

In my Master studies, using Bayesian statistics, I found that heavy chains of antibodies confer DNA recognition properties, opening new doors for research in systemic lupus erythematosus, a medical condition that is characterized by the presence of anti-DNA antibodies.
