About

I started in the field of Bioinformatics in 2008 in my undergraduation studies, when I learned perl programming, Latex and Unix systems. Since then, I tackled problems in different biological systems. I am currently analysing data of laboratory medicine, focusing on detecting early signs of anemia.

In my PhD studies, I developed different methods and algorithms to study evolution of non-coding RNAs. Most prominently, I developed the SSS-test, the first test in the scientific community that can detect positive selection in ncRNA structures. With the SSS-test, it is possible to detect fast-evolving local strucures in ncRNAs.

In my Master studies, I used Bayesian statistics to find that heavy chains confer DNA recognition properties to antibodies, opening new doors for research in systemic lupus erythematosus, a medical condition that produces anti-DNA antibodies.

In addition to scientific findings, I specialized in communicating proficiently with researchers from different fields, backgrounds and cultures. I worked with collaborators from Germany, Brazil, Mexico, Canada and India.

In order to be productive and objective in my work, I have developed my strategy for documentation, backup and planning. This is based on a personal lab book, which I write in Latex. I also use Git daily, a version control tool, for updating my lab book and my codes. As an example of how efficient my framework is, I managed to write my PhD dissertation in only 170 hours, which is an extremely short time for such a task.
